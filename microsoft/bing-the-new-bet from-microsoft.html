Bing, Microsoft's new bet to destroy Google, integrate AI into its search engine.

Microsoft continues to take steps in its plan to integrate artificial intelligence models into its browser. Last October we learned about Microsoft Bing's Image Creator, its integration of DALL-E 2 within the search engine. The investment of 1,000 million dollars in OpenAI comes hand in hand with this type of integration, and the next one will be ChatGPT.

A surprisingly intelligent search

Microsoft is getting ready to integrate ChatGPT into Bing search. The company hopes that a first version can be released before March, thus anticipating Google's possible plans with Chrome. It will be the next big step after the integration of DALL-E 2 within Bing.

The operation of ChatGPT within Bing will be similar to what we would expect from this type of artificial intelligence in a search engine. Your job will be to provide answers supported by AI, with sources known to ChatGPT such as link lists, keyword suggestions related to search and more. Microsoft wants the answer to continue to be given by the Bing engine and the AI ​​to be a support, since ChatGPT is not designed to offer real-time crawled information from web pages.

The integration of ChatGPT in Bing will make it necessary to refine the veracity of its answers, Microsoft also having to solve the challenge of response speed. A search engine does not require browsing through its entire data set for each query made (indexing algorithms are for this), while AI models work by analyzing the data on which they have been trained.
